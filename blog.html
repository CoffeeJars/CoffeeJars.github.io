You might have noticed that the design of my blog has once again changed. I can safely say that this will be the final iteration. I want to take a minute to lay out some of my thoughts on blogging and design.

The obvious question, “why update a blog in the first place?” Well, it takes a certain kind of person. I don’t do it for money. I am under no delusions that enough of an audience would ever want to read my fragmented sentences, or keep up to date with my life, injuries, job, worries, training, reading, etc.

I blog because I enjoy it.

I love writing; I love making webpages. Blogging hits that perfect overlap in the Venn diagram.

I wanted this website to be a safe haven, a shelter, a pirates’ cove for the written word. These days’ language has taken a back seat, hidden behind videos and pictures and audio and god knows what else. There is a quiet beauty in words. The mystery of language. Reading isn’t like watching a video, you don’t have information chewed up and spit back out for you. When you read you play the diver, in a reef of coral words, you play the archeologist dusting off sentences and phrases, labeling them carefully, subject verb article noun. Reading is hacking through a jungle, carving a path from intro to conclusion.

I wanted this to somehow be reflected on my site. To place the written word above all else. This is why my blog is text only.
