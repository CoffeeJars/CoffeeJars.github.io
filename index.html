Me

I am an aspiring Computer Engineer in the 3rd year at UConn. I am proficient in Python and have various work & hobby experience using it:

  • As part of Berrah Lab I used Python to assist research on multiphoton processes in the X-Ray regime. Specifically, I wrote a constant fraction discriminator to accurately measure the time of flight of argon cations. I also wrote code for data analysis, HDF5 file access, and plotting.
  • As part of Elena Dormidontova's Computational Soft Matter Physics Group I wrote a liquid crystal simulation with Alice Cong. The simulation was implemented using the Metropolis–Hastings Monte-Carlo algorithm. I also wrote code to visualize accepted steps in the algorithm using VMD.
  • During the summer of 2019 I interned at RACE Coastal Engineering. I wrote Python functions to generate heat maps from lat/long coordinates to be displayed on Google Earth. I also wrote a GUI application to retrieve historical aerial photography.

I am comfortable with scheme, am learning C++, have used VHDL for coursework, and know a handful of markup languages.

These days I spend my free time exercising, reading, programming, playing games, and with my dog, Nemo.

I once competed in powerlifting and have a 1096 lbs total. I have also completed a half marathon.
