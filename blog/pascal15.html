I decided to tackle another project Euler question, #15. Turns out I have no knowledge whatsoever on combinatorics. Yikes. After a quick wikipedia skim I stumbled upon a picture of Pascal's triangle, and realized that the number of unique lattice paths seemed to fall along a medial line. While I might not exactly understand why this is the case, I quickly wrote a python script to output Pascal's triangle.

If we define the top of the triangle as the 0th level, it follows that the the number of unique lattice paths for a grid of n by n size will be the central number on the n*2 level of Pascal's triangle.
